# Quick Start: CoPro Calculator for Filmmakers

## What This Tool Does

You tell it about your film. It tells you:

1. **Which countries** can give you financing (tax credits, rebates, grants)
2. **How much** you could recover (e.g., "€700K back on a €3.5M budget")
3. **What you need to do** to qualify (where to shoot, who to hire, what tests to pass)
4. **Why** each number is reliable (every figure links to an official government source)

---

## The Filmmaker's Three Key Questions

### 1. "How should I structure my film to get the best financing?"

The calculator shows you **the knobs you can turn**:
- Where to shoot (each country % affects available incentives)
- Who are your creative team (nationalities matter for co-production treaties)
- When to do post-production (which country, affects which incentives apply)
- Budget split (how much on shooting vs. post vs. other)

**Example**: You have a €3.5M film, thinking of shooting 50% in France, 30% in Germany, 20% elsewhere.
- The tool shows: "TRIP in France could give you €700K" (if conditions met)
- But if you move to "60% France, 20% Germany": "You could unlock Germany's incentive too, maybe €500K more"
- Or: "If you shift post-production to the UK, the AVEC credit adds another £300K"

### 2. "Can I actually qualify for these incentives?"

The calculator breaks down **what each incentive requires**:
- Minimum spend in the country
- Minimum percentage of crew that must be local
- Cultural tests (British content requirements, Australian narrative focus, etc.)
- Shoot-day requirements (how many days must be in-country)

**Example**: UK's AVEC credit looks great (34% rebate) — but:
- Requires cultural test score of 17/31
- Requires 10% of budget to be UK spend
- If your film is purely American content, you fail the cultural test

The tool tells you upfront, so you don't waste time pursuing a dead-end.

### 3. "Who should I co-produce with?"

The calculator matches **bilateral and multilateral treaties**:
- UK + France coproduction? → Use UK-France treaty (can structure minority coproducer roles)
- Multi-country (France + Germany + UK)? → Use multilateral European treaty
- Each treaty has rules: minimum shares, creative requirements, which countries lead

**Example**:
- France-led with minority German co-producer → Unlocks both countries' incentives + treaty benefits
- But the German co-producer must contribute ≥20% (per treaty rule) and have creative control
- The tool shows: "If 20% German, you unlock €1.2M in combined incentives vs. €700K France-only"

## How to Use It (Two Modes)

### Mode 1: Guided Interview (Easiest)

1. Click **"Guided interview"** tab
2. Chat with the AI: "It's a €2M feature, shooting in France and Spain"
3. AI asks follow-up questions (What's the genre? Director's nationality? Budget split?)
4. AI fills in the form for you
5. Click **"Analyze"** → get scenarios

**Best for**: First time, just exploring, have a treatment/synopsis to upload.

### Mode 2: Manual Form (Precise Control)

1. Click **"Manual form"** tab
2. Fill in:
   - **Title & format** (feature, documentary, series, animation)
   - **Budget & currency** (EUR, GBP, USD, etc.)
   - **Production stage** (development, production, post-production)
   - **Shoot locations** (e.g., 60% France, 40% Germany)
   - **Creative team nationalities** (director, producers, key crew)
   - **Production company location**
   - **Budget breakdown** (advanced: % on shooting, post, other)
   - **Flexibility options** (Are you willing to adjust to unlock more incentives?)
3. Click **"Analyze"** → get scenarios

**Best for**: Precise control, iterating different scenarios, sharing with partners.

## Understanding the Results

### The Scenario Card

Each result shows:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│   France TRIP + Domestic Credit     │
│                                     │
│  Estimated incentive recovery      │
│   €1,225,000  (~35% of budget)     │
│                                     │
│  ✓ France 60% / EU co-pro structure│
│  • 30% tax rebate (TRIP)            │
│  • 25% tax credit (Domestic)        │
│  • Requires: min €250K FR spend,    │
│    local producer, 5+ shoot days   │
│  • Mutual exclusivity: Choose one  │
│                                     │
│  [Show calculation] [Learn more]    │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**What this means**:
- **Title**: The scenario name (country + which incentives)
- **Estimated incentive recovery**: €1.2M back if all conditions met
- **Percentage**: That's 35% of your €3.5M budget (strong financing source)
- **Checkmarks**: What your film meets
- **Bullets**: Individual incentives in this scenario
- **Requirements**: What you need to do
- **Mutual exclusivity**: Can't stack these two — you pick one based on your structure
- **[Show calculation]**: Click to see exactly how we got to €1.2M

### Click "Learn More" or "Show Calculation"

Each number is **linked to an official source**:
- `source_url`: The government page (CNC, BFI, etc.)
- `source_description`: Which specific section ("Art. 220 quaterdecies")
- `notes`: What the rule actually says

**Why this matters**: You can share this with your accountant, co-producers, or financiers and say: "This isn't my guess — it's from the official government source."

## Key Eligibility Levers (What You Control)

### 1. Shoot Location %
- Each country's incentives have minimum % requirements
- Moving shoot from 40% France to 60% might unlock a higher incentive tier
- Trade-off: logistics, crew availability, budget

### 2. Production Company Location
- Where your lead company is registered affects which treaties apply
- France-registered company → access French incentives + EU treaties
- Trade-off: legal/tax complexity

### 3. Crew Nationality
- Some incentives require % of crew to be local
- Some treaties require creative team (director, producer) from the co-producing countries
- Trade-off: who you hire

### 4. Budget Timing & Split
- When you spend affects which incentives apply (development, production, post)
- Where you spend (shooting country vs. post-production country) matters
- Trade-off: schedule, location decisions

### 5. Format & Stage
- Feature fiction, documentary, series, animation have different incentive access
- Development, production, post-production stages have different incentives
- Trade-off: none, but helps you pick the right program for your stage

### 6. Cultural Requirements
- Some incentives have cultural tests (British content, Australian narrative focus, etc.)
- Passing = access higher rebates; failing = excluded entirely
- Trade-off: creative compromise?

## Workflow: From Idea to Financing

1. **Draft your concept** (genre, budget range, initial locations, team)
2. **Use the guided interview** to explore what's available
3. **See which scenarios look best** (high incentive %, few requirements, meets your vision)
4. **Switch to manual mode** to iterate (what if we shoot 70% in France instead of 50%)
5. **Evaluate trade-offs**:
   - "To unlock the €1.2M scenario, I need to:
     - Hire a French producer (doable, already know someone)
     - Shoot minimum 5 days in France (my plan had 10, so ✓)
     - Meet the cultural test (my film is Franco-British co-pro, should pass)"
6. **Share the results** with:
   - Co-producers (which territories would work best)
   - Financiers (here's €1.2M we could recover, backed by official sources)
   - Accountants/lawyers (so they can validate and plan the legal structure)
7. **Make your decision**: This scenario becomes part of your financing plan

## Red Flags to Watch

### ⚠️ "I see a big number — but is it really available to me?"

**Always check the requirements** under each scenario:
- "This incentive requires cultural test" — does your film pass?
- "Min €500K spend in country" — can you budget that?
- "Local producer required" — do you have one?

The tool shows what you *could* get. Reality requires matching the fine print.

### ⚠️ "The source says 30% but the official site says 25%"

**The tool is only as good as its data.** Each record has a `last_verified` date.
- Green (verified < 6 months): Trust it
- Yellow (6–12 months old): Verify manually with the government source
- Red (>12 months old): Definitely check the government website before relying on it

When in doubt, click the source link and verify directly.

### ⚠️ "I don't see my country"

The tool covers 28+ countries and growing. If yours isn't listed:
1. Check if there's a **bilateral treaty** with a covered country (even without local incentives)
2. Or: **Propose an update** (click "Report issue" on the results) — help us add your country

## Tips for Success

1. **Start broad, then narrow**: Try the guided interview first. It's conversational and helps you think through what matters.

2. **Iterate**: Use manual mode to ask "what if" questions. Each scenario is a different structure.

3. **Involve early**: Share results with producers, co-producers, and your accountant before committing. The financing structure affects deal structure.

4. **Verify with the source**: The tool is a guide, not a legal document. Official sources are always authoritative.

5. **Plan for change**: Government incentive rates and rules change. Double-check before finalizing contracts.

6. **Think partnerships**: Often the best financing comes from finding the right co-producer. Use this tool to identify which partnerships make sense financially.
